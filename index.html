Databases of Early Latin Manuscripts (DELM)

DELM is a network of researchers and research projects that aims to facilitate data exchange between databases describing early Latin manuscripts.

The network was founded in May 2023. Its co-ordinators are:

  • Immo Warntjes (Trinity College, Dublin): e-mail | web
  • Pádraic Moran (University of Galway): e-mail | web

Relevant projects

  • Bibliotheca Legum: A Database on Carolingian Secular Law Texts
  • Biblissima+ (Observatory of Written Cultures, from Clay to Print)
  • Computus in the Carolingian Age
  • Earlier Latin Manuscripts
  • Innovating Knowledge: Isidore’s Etymologiae in the Carolingian Period
  • IrCaBriTT: Ireland and Carolingian Brittany: Texts and Transmission
  • Jordanus: An International Catalogue of Medieval Scientific Manuscripts
  • MIrA: Manuscripts with Irish Associations
  • PASSIM: Patristic Sermons in the Middle Ages
  • PeopleAndWriting:People, Script and Ideas in the Iberian Peninsula (c. 900-1200)
  • Renewing the Carolingian Renaissance (750–900)
  • Searobend (Linked Metadata for English-Language Texts 1000–1300)

Shared digital resources

See our GitHub site: https://github.com/DELM-net

Participate

If you have a database of Latin manuscripts (ideally online), contact either or both of our co-ordinators above to have your project listed above.

Join our mailing list for general discussion and information about meetings. Send a blank e-mail to delm+subscribe@googlegroups.com. When you receive a response, send a reply to confirm.
