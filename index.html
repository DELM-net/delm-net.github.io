DELM is a network of researchers and research projects working on databases of early Latin manuscripts that aims to facilitate data exchange, co-ordination and collaboration.

The network was founded in early 2024, following a meeting of manuscript researchers in Dublin in May 2023. Its co-ordinators are Immo Warntjes (e-mail) and Pádraic Moran (e-mail).

Mailing list

Join our mailing list for general discussion and for information about future meetings.

Instructions: send a blank e-mail to delm+subscribe@googlegroups.com. When you receive a response, just reply to confirm. (Clicking the link does not always seem to work. If you don't see a response, check your Spam folder. If you have further problems, contact Pádraic Moran above.)

Meetings

We aim to meet once or twice yearly (in person and/or online) to discuss research issues of common interest. Our next meeting is planned for Tuesday 13 May 2025.

Everyone is welcome to participate. Details will be circulated via our mailing list (see above).

Some relevant projects

  • Bibliotheca Legum: A Database on Carolingian Secular Law Texts
  • Biblissima+ (Observatory of Written Cultures, from Clay to Print)
  • Clavis canonum – Selected Canon Law Collections, ca. 500–1234
  • Computus in the Carolingian Age
  • Corpus of Early Medieval Latin Medicine
  • Digital Latin Library
  • Earlier Latin Manuscripts
  • e-codices – Virtual Manuscript Library of Switzerland
  • Fragmentarium: Laboratory for Medieval Manuscript Fragments
  • Innovating Knowledge: Isidore’s Etymologiae in the Carolingian Period
  • IrCaBriTT: Ireland and Carolingian Brittany: Texts and Transmission
  • Jordanus: An International Catalogue of Medieval Scientific Manuscripts
  • Marginal Scholarship: The Practice of Learning in the Early Middle Ages (c. 800–c. 1000)
  • Minuscule Texts: Marginalized Voices in Early Medieval Latin Culture c. 700–c. 1000
  • MIrA: Manuscripts with Irish Associations
  • PASSIM: Patristic Sermons in the Middle Ages
  • PeopleAndWriting: People, Script and Ideas in the Iberian Peninsula (c. 900-1200)
  • Renewing the Carolingian Renaissance (750–900)
  • Searobend (Linked Metadata for English-Language Texts 1000–1300)

If you have a database of Latin manuscripts (ideally online), contact either or both of our co-ordinators above to include your project in the list above.
